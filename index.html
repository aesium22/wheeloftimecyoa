World Notes

The Wheel turns, and Ages come and pass, leaving memories that become legend. Now, at the end of the Third Age, the Pattern stirs violently. On November 11, 1000 NE, at the hidden battlefield of Shadar Logoth, the taint was cleansed from Saidin. For the first time in thousands of years, male channelers can touch the One Power without inevitable madness.


Yet the world is about to enter the darkest period of the Third Age. Kingdoms fracture. The Seanchan tighten their grip across the western lands. The White Tower struggles to regain its authority. The Black Tower rises, a new power among the ruins of the old.


Of every thousand born, only about 1% possess the ability to channel. Of these, perhaps one in 10,000 is strong enough to be noticed, trained, and feared.


You are one of these rare souls — born with an above-average strength in the Power, destined to make your mark upon an Age trembling on the edge of catastrophe.

Difficulty / Role in the Pattern

Your connection to the Pattern determines the dangers you will face.
Involved in the Pattern +25 points
  • Fate actively weaves around you. Heroes, villains, and Forsaken will cross your path.
  • Major threats and opportunities will seek you out.
  • Expect to be drawn into great battles, ancient plots, and world-changing events.
Ignored by the Pattern +15 points
  • You live quieter, outside the direct pull of prophecy.
  • Most dangers must be sought out deliberately.
  • Greater safety, but lesser natural chances at fame, fortune, or catastrophe.

Starting Place

The lands of the Westlands offer many beginnings. Choose your birthplace carefully; it shapes your early skills, alliances, and dangers.
🌊 Seanchan Empire +6 points

You were raised among the Seanchan conquerors.

You are either:

  • A sul'dam whose talents awakened.
  • An escaped damane hiding your identity.

Advantages:

Rare access to Seanchan military knowledge, Traveling secrets, and exotic training methods.

Disadvantages:

Hunted by seekers and slave-catchers if discovered.

🏰 Andoran Borderlands +0 points

Raised near the powerful kingdom of Andor, close to Tar Valon's sphere of influence.

Familiar with noble politics, minor wars, and White Tower presence.

Advantages:

Easier access to formal training, patronage, and safety.

🌊 Illian +0 points

Born in a bustling trade city of sea, ships, and coin.

Loose channeling oversight allows freedom — and danger.

Advantages:

Access to rare goods, diverse fighting styles, and mercenary networks.

♟️ Cairhien +0 points

Raised in the heart of Daes Dae'mar — the Great Game of noble intrigue.

You grew up surrounded by plots within plots.

Advantages:

Naturally gifted at politics, espionage, and maneuvering between powers.

No White Tower or Black Tower starting option: you must first find your path to them through the Pattern.

2️⃣

Starting Training Level

Your education will dramatically affect your early strength.
🔥 Untrained +5 points
  • You have no formal instruction. If you pick Wanderer, you have no channeling knowledge at all.
  • Your instincts guide your channeling.

Advantages:

Full creative freedom. Learn lost or rare weaves more easily.

Disadvantages:

Poor control, slower spellcasting without focus, and risks of burning out.

📚 Graduated from Tower Training +0 points

You have completed core training at one of the major schools:

  • White Tower (Aes Sedai)
  • Black Tower (Asha'man)

You have basic mastery over:

  • Standard battle weaves.
  • Basic Healing techniques.
  • Opening and closing Traveling gateways.
  • Setting simple wards.

Advantages:

Highly stable spellcasting, formal recognition, easier access to allies and resources.

Disadvantages:

Political entanglements; enemies and rivals may already know your name.

🚶 Wanderer +3 points

You grew up outside any major channeler structure. This might include:

  • Wisdom from a remote village.
  • Windfinder of the Sea Folk.
  • Tairen city channeler hiding in the slums.
  • Two Rivers Talent with deep but wild roots.

Advantages:

Greater creativity and flexibility. Closer bond to non-channeler cultures.

Disadvantages:

No early formal training; must survive first trials by instinct.

3️⃣

Affinities

You are naturally gifted in certain types of channeling. Affinities make learning related weaves easier, faster, and more powerful.

Pick up to 1 Affinity for free. Each additional Affinity costs 2 points.

💙 Healing Free/-2 points
  • Natural ability to repair injuries, cure disease, and stabilize critical wounds.
  • Healing is faster, more complete, and costs less strength compared to ordinary channelers.
  • Unlocks specialized Healing weaves at lower skill thresholds.
⚔️ Combat Weaving Free/-2 points
  • Expertise in destruction, battle wards, and offensive weaves.
  • Stronger fire, earth, and air-based attacks.
  • Easier combination of multiple weaves in live combat.
🌀 Traveling and Skimming Free/-2 points
  • Faster mastery over opening gateways and traveling between locations.
  • Can open larger, more stable portals with less effort.
  • Minor chance to invent rare movement techniques.
🛡️ Warding and Detection Free/-2 points
  • Craft powerful protective barriers, alarm wards, and anti-scrying fields.
  • Easier time detecting hidden presences, masked channelers, or dream-walkers.
🔗 Binding and Stilling Free/-2 points
  • Strength in shield weaves (blocking another's ability to channel) and immobilization.
  • Easier time cutting someone off from the One Power if sufficiently strong.
  • Boosts defensive techniques dramatically.
🌩️ Weather Working Free/-2 points
  • Influence local weather patterns: rain, storms, calming winds.
  • Can resist minor environmental hazards (cold, heat, storms) with ambient weaves.
  • With training, capable of major-scale weather manipulation in circles.
👁️ Illusion and Masking Free/-2 points
  • Talent in disguise, concealment, and appearance-shifting.
  • Stronger versions of Mirror of Mists, masking gender, hiding strength.
  • Passive bonus against detection by Wards or the keen senses of Wolfbrothers.
💤 Dreamwalking Free/-2 points
  • Easier and safer entry into Tel'aran'rhiod, the World of Dreams.
  • Ability to resist some dream manipulation or nightmares.
  • Unlocks rare dream-weaves and defense against Dreamspike traps.
🔮 Ter'angreal Creation and Use Free/-2 points
  • Can instinctively activate, decipher, or cautiously test ter'angreal devices.
  • Minor talent for constructing new minor ter'angreal given time and resources.
  • Lower risk when experimenting with dangerous artifacts.

Affinity grants you easier growth in your chosen path — but specializing too narrowly leaves weaknesses.

4️⃣

Special Spells

Special spells are elite signature weaves within broader Affinities. They represent major narrative and mechanical advantages.
  • Cast ~2x faster than normal versions.
  • Stronger effect (damage, healing, size, potency).
  • Resistance against disruption.
  • Access to rare or secret weaves otherwise locked.

You may purchase as many Special Spells as points allow.

🔥 Firestorm -4 points
Unleash a localized inferno, engulfing a battlefield in fire and air. Devastates unprepared foes.
💖 Sever Healing -5 points
Heal someone who has been cut off from the One Power. Rare, difficult, and life-altering.
🌌 Rapid Gateway -5 points
Open stable gateways instantly, even under duress. Critical for retreat or rapid deployment.
🛡️ Inviolable Ward -4 points
Creates a powerful defensive barrier that resists intrusion, attack, and scrying.
🛑 Chain Shield -3 points
Shield multiple channelers at once. Excellent for crowd control, less effective on elites.
🌩️ Stormcall -3 points
Summon a thunderstorm across 1–2 miles. Dangerous to both enemies and terrain.
🕵️ True Mask of Mirrors -2 points
Advanced illusions — scent, voice, touch. Highly convincing to most observers.
🌠 Worldwalker -3 points
Move between Tel’aran’rhiod and the waking world. Extremely rare and dangerous.
🛠️ Ter’angreal Crafter -6 points
Construct basic ter’angreal. Takes time and resources, but unlocks custom magical tools.

Unique Items and Special Abilities

The Third Age is a time of relics — many lost, some rediscovered. You have access to rare ter'angreal and unique abilities. Choose wisely.

📦 Personal Ter'angreal

Choose from the list below:

(Lasts several hours once activated.)

Sifter Lens -2 points

(Effective against illusions, maskings, and dream influences.)

Pocket Haven -6 points

Opens a small, stable shelter in the World of Dreams or nearby "frozen" time bubbles.

(Allows 6 hours of protected rest; extremely rare and prized.)

Luck Charm -5 points

A token that subtly shifts probability in your favor during high-stakes weaves (combat, Healing, duels).

(Not absolute, but adds a strong edge.)
